Understanding the DLS Method

Resources Concept

The DLS method is based on the concept that teams have two resources to make runs: overs remaining and wickets in hand. When a match is interrupted, the resources available to one or both teams are affected.

Resource Percentage

Each combination of overs and wickets has a resource percentage. A team starting their innings has 100% resources (all overs and all wickets). As they lose wickets or overs are reduced, their resource percentage decreases.

Target Calculation

The revised target is calculated based on the resources available to both teams. If Team 2 has fewer resources than Team 1, their target is adjusted proportionally to ensure fairness.

How It Works

Understanding the Duckworth-Lewis-Stern Method

Resource-Based Calculation

The DLS method uses a resource table that considers overs remaining and wickets in hand to calculate fair targets in rain-affected matches.

Mid-Innings Interruptions

When Team 2's innings is interrupted, the calculator adjusts the target based on resources lost due to reduced overs.

Professional vs Standard Edition

Important Note: This calculator uses the publicly available DLS Standard Edition table. Professional T20 leagues (IPL, BBL, etc.) use the proprietary DLS Professional Edition with different resource values. Results may differ from official targets by 5-15 runs for T20 matches.
